Homework 2: Shape

I wanted to implement drawing apps in different "spaces," challenging myself to interpret what "space" means. I was interested in the drawing apps challenge, but I wanted to take it a step further. This ended up being more of a coding challenge for myself to create different, complex, and unusual spaces for users to draw shapes (or on shapes). After we experimented with simple 2D drawing apps in class, I tried to imagine what other drawing tools I could create in Processing that afford modes of drawing not traditionally offered. So I ended up testing out drawing in 3D space in two sketches, as well as a more simplistic, literal take on space for the third sketch.

Draw in 3D Decorate a Cake Constellations
Homework 3: Color

For this challenge, I wanted to experiment with color blending and discover all the different ways to blend color using p5.js. I played around with lerpColor() and hue to create different effects across my sketches. I wanted to make all of them interactive, allowing the user to control how the colors are blended and/or how the blended colors are displayed. Some are simple and some are very complicated to get the color blending just right for the perfect color experience.

Color Blender Sunrise Sunset Rainbow Scratch Art
Homework 4: Motion

For this challenge, I wanted to use different forms of motion and interaction to express the different states of bipolar disorder. I created sketches for depression, stability, and mania. Since the person with bipolar disorder experiences these states, I wanted the person interacting with this sketch to drive the experience of the motion. The idea is for the user to feel the emotions as a result of the interactive motion experience.

Depression Stable Mania
